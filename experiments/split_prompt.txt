You are tasked with analyzing an audio recording of an academic lecture. The lecture covers several subtopics. Your job is to:
1. Come up with a concise title (no more than 80 characters) that captures the overall theme or main subject of the lecture.
2. Identify natural break points in the lecture to split it into shorter segments based on subtopic changes. Each segment should:
    - Focus on a single subtopic;
    - Be between 2 and 4 minutes long (120–240 seconds);
    - Have a logical beginning and end (i.e., not cut off mid‑sentence or in the middle of explaining a concept).

Provide your response in the following format:
- On the first line, write the proposed title.
- On each subsequent line, list a single timestamp (in seconds from the start of the recording) indicating where a split should occur. Provide the exact timestamp in whole seconds (e.g., 123, not 120 or 123.5). Ensure splits occur at natural pauses, never mid‑sentence. Prioritize topic coherence over strict duration; if a subtopic runs 4:10, split at the next logical break even if it exceeds 4 minutes. Aim for 1‑second precision—avoid rounding to 10s or 30s intervals.
